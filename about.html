Introduction about UK

The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, commonly known as the United Kingdom or Great Britain, or simply UK is a sovereign state in Western Europe. It consists of 4 countries, England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland, and a few small islands. The capital and the largest city of the UK is London.The UK is an island country along with Republic of Ireland, and it shares its land borderds with the same. The United Kingdom is surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean, with the North Sea to its east, the English Channel to its south and the Celtic Sea to its south-south-west. United Kingdom is the 78th-largest sovereign state in the world and the 11th-largest in Europe. It is also the 21st-most populous country, with an estimated 65.1 million inhabitants. It belongs to Europe

The United Kingdom is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary democracy. The monarch is Queen Elizabeth II, who has reigned since 6 February 1952, and the Prime Minister is Theresa May, assumed office on 11 July 2016. The British Democracy is responsible for choosing a leader for the Uk, who is chosen by the people, through voting. The monarch, though cannot interfere directly in the administrative matters, she represents the country in many important affairs, and also is considered the head of the society. The Parliamentary form (Legislature)is divided into 2 houses - House of Commons (Lower house) and House of Lords (Upper house). Any law has to get the agreement of both these houses.

The United Kingdom is a developed country and has the world's fifth-largest economy by nominal GDP and ninth-largest economy by purchasing power parity. The UK is considered to have a high-income economy and is categorised as very high in the Human Development Index, ranking 16th in the world. It was the world's first industrialised country and the world's foremost power during the 19th and early 20th centuries. The UK remains a great power with considerable economic, cultural, military, scientific and political influence internationally. UK is still one of the most considerably strongest powers in the world and the British Forces (Her Majesty's Armed Forces) keep the nation highly secure. UK is also the home to important populations of grey seals, and rare bat species, and many more endangered species. It has signed many referendums and agreements, and is also one of the most important members of some world-known welfare organistions. The Royal Society of Sciences in London makes important contribuitions in the field of Science every year, and the old Brit scientists have already formulated so many Maths theories. Even after being such a busy country full of hustle-bustle, it is known to be one of the most courruption-free countries.
